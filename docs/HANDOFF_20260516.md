## Headline findings

### Finding 1: Cycle 5 is the correct manuscript reporting horizon

Cycle 1 baseline corresponds to year 2004 (baseline_year 1999 + 1 cycle × 5 yr); the RPA snapshots are vintage 2020 to 2022. Cycle 5 (year 2024) is the RPA-comparable vintage. Pulling cycle 5 values from `ci_summaries.csv` directly (rather than linear-interpolating between c1 and c15) reveals:

| State | c1 vol | c5 vol (RPA-comparable) | c15 vol | FIA all-live | c5 vs FIA |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| ME r21 RCP 4.5 | 1,533 | 1,749 | 1,168 | 1,581 | **+11%** |
| MN p1 RCP 4.5 | 1,241 | 1,408 | 944 | 1,183 | **+19%** |
| WA p1 RCP 4.5 | 3,133 | 3,012 | 1,310 | 4,318 | **-30%** |
| GA p1 RCP 4.5 | 1,326 | 1,984 | 2,067 | 2,759 | **-28%** |

The trajectory is NOT linear; volume grows from c1 to c5 then declines toward c15 as TPA artifacts emerge. The original RPA comparison memo had GA at -50% (cycle 1 vintage) but at cycle 5 it's -28%. WA stays around -30% across both vintages. ME and MN slightly over-predict by 11-19% at the cycle 5 vintage, consistent with the per-acre live-tree volume comparison.

**Manuscript implication:** report cycle 5 values, not cycle 1 baseline or cycle 15 endpoints.

### Finding 2: TPA accumulates without bound after cycle 5

At cycle 15, TPA ranges from 13,000 to 51,000 trees per acre across the four states under No_harvest, which is biophysically impossible (mature natural forests rarely exceed 5,000 trees/ac in dense regenerating stands).

| State | c1 TPA | c5 TPA | c15 BAU TPA | c15 NoHarv TPA | c15 ratio |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| ME r21 | 742 | 946 | 23,390 | 41,416 | 56× |
| MN p1 | 543 | 613 | 5,702 | 13,028 | 24× |
| WA p1 | 340 | 510 | 15,126 | **51,178** | **151×** |
| GA p1 | 498 | 606 | 4,782 | 23,059 | 46× |

**Root cause:** `apply_sdimax_cap()` in `cem_pipeline_patch/06_projection_engine.R` lines 742-763 applies `sdi_ratio` to BA, volume, drybio, and carbon when SDI exceeds SDImax, but does not apply it to `proj_tpa`. SDI = TPA × (QMD/10)^1.605, so reducing BA without reducing TPA implicitly grows QMD (the model claims trees get bigger as SDI climbs). The biological reality is the opposite: at SDImax, mortality removes small trees first while survivors grow bigger. The current code is mathematically inconsistent.

**Cycle 5 is safe:** TPA at cycle 5 (510 to 946) is plausible. The blow-up emerges between cycles 7 and 10. **Confirms cycle 5 manuscript horizon recommendation.**

**Layer 4 patch (proposed but not applied):**

```r
# In cem_pipeline_patch/06_projection_engine.R apply_sdimax_cap()
# Currently at lines 754-758:
proj_BA       = proj_BA       * sdi_ratio,
proj_volcfnet = proj_volcfnet * sdi_ratio,
# ... etc

# ADD:
proj_tpa      = proj_tpa      * sdi_ratio
```

Or the cleaner sqrt distribution variant documented in TRAJECTORY_DIAGNOSTIC_20260516.md.

The minimal one-line patch will probably reduce projected vol and carbon at the cap (since the cap is applied multiplicatively to both TPA and BA), worsening the GA and WA -28 to -30% RPA gap. The sqrt variant splits the impact and may be the better path. Deciding which to land is the next session decision.

### Finding 3: WA No_harvest declines uniquely

Among 4 states × 2 RCPs, exactly one combination (WA, both RCPs) produces volume DECLINING under No_harvest:

| State | c1 No_harv vol | c15 No_harv vol | Δ |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| ME r21 4.5 | 1,608 | 1,958 | +22% |
| MN p1 4.5 | 1,302 | 1,758 | +35% |
| WA p1 4.5 | 3,279 | **1,946** | **-41%** |
| GA p1 4.5 | 1,378 | 4,196 | +204% |

Most likely cause: WA `state_constants.csv` has `fire_baseline_per_cycle = 0.060` (10× ME) and the disturbance model applies `fire_red = 0.40` (60% biomass loss per event). Compounded over 15 cycles this can produce net volume loss even without harvest.

WA fire diagnostic (job 9695159) running with `--fire_amp_mult 0.5` to halve fire intensity. If WA No_harvest then grows (like the other states), we confirm disturbance compounding is the mechanism. If WA still declines, look at SDImax or donor dynamics next.

### Finding 4: Layer 2 patch validated at 10-sim scale

The 10-sim follow-up (job 9579959) ran ME --use_maine_econ at n_sims 10, cycles 5. Results:

| Cycle | gr_ratio (95% CI) | Harvest rate |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0.425 (0.416, 0.433) | 83.6% |
| 2 | 0.981 (0.958, 1.011) | 45.3% |
| 3 | 0.990 (0.968, 1.013) | 28.5% |
| 4 | 0.946 (0.895, 1.002) | 20.3% |
| 5 | 0.959 (0.852, 1.020) | 14.4% |

gr_ratio converges to ~1.0 (sustained yield) with tight CIs. The 83.6% cycle 1 harvest rate persists at n_sims=10, so it's not smoke noise but a real Wear 2025 logit recalibration debt that the prior bug had been masking. Decays to 14.4% by cycle 5, suggesting the calibration debt is a one-time transient. A 100-sim full Maine econ rerun would confirm.
