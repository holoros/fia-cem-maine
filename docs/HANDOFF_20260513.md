**The most important finding of this session arc is a unit bug in my analysis tooling, not the projection.** My hindcast script and validation template treated `proj_carbon` as kg/ac when it is actually lb/ac (FIA pounds per tree from `CARBON_AG`, summed by TPA_UNADJ). This inflated statewide totals by 2.2x and produced spurious +65 to +145 percent hindcast bias findings. After fixing the conversion factor to LB_TO_MMT, the ME r21 diagnostic confirms cycle 1 (2004) proj/obs ratio collapses from 2.20 to 1.00 — the projection's baseline is essentially exact, and the canonical ME r11 hindcast performance (RMSE ~16 MMT, bias ~-2 MMT) is reproduced.

The retraction: the projections were never +65 to +145 percent over predicting. My script was. The fix is in `259b481`. SLURM jobs 9602288 (hindcast rerun) and 9603371 (revalidation rerun) will produce corrected memos in the next 50 minutes. The original validation memos' statewide carbon totals (e.g., MN 586 TgC, WA 1377 TgC) are 2.2x inflated; corrected values are MN ~266, WA ~624, GA ~397 TgC.

### Methodological reading of the consistent flags

- **Harvest rate clusters at 9.8 to 9.9 percent across all four states.** Smoke baselines varied (MN 11.6, WA 16.7, GA 19.9), suggesting the smoke runs were taken at different code states or scenario weights. The production cluster at ~9.9 percent reflects `--fixed_harvest_rate 0.10` × scenario_Q ~0.99 calibration, then attenuated slightly by `--use_owner_balanced` rescaling. Recommend rewriting the smoke bounds to a single tight band of [8.5, 11.5] for BAU baseline harvest rate across states. This is methodological behavior, not a bug.
- **Statewide carbon overshoots the upper bound by 4 to 8 percent for WA and GA.** Per acre carbon for each is inside its own bound; the overshoot accumulates through the forest area multiplier. Worth a literature check against published WA and GA statewide AGC estimates before manuscript inclusion; if production matches published estimates, widen the bound to [600, 1500] TgC.
- **gr_ratio cycle 1 at 0.0070 to 0.0120 across all six.** Post Layer 1 fix the values are correct in magnitude order. The Layer 2 patch landed this session will push gr_ratio toward 0.5 to 3.0 on the next econ rerun, but the multistate runs use `--no_econ` and are unaffected.
